Beyond the Research

A little about
life outside work

I'm a PhD student in Atlanta, originally from Iran. Outside of economics, I find joy in journaling, music, movement, and quietly showing up for others — often when no one is watching.

About Me

I grew up in Iran and moved to Atlanta to pursue my PhD at Georgia State University. Living between two very different cultures has shaped how I think about inequality, mobility, and opportunity — themes that run through my research as much as my life.

I start my mornings early, usually with journaling and meditation before the day takes over. I believe in doing good quietly — helping others not for recognition, but because it feels right. Golf keeps me patient, the piano keeps me present, and the gym keeps me grounded.

I'm a lifelong reader drawn to books about growth, meaning, and the human spirit. I'm always happy to connect — whether about research, books, or life.
