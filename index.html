Research

Working Papers

Research Focus
Applied microeconomics of labor markets, health policy, and technological change, with an emphasis on causal inference and policy-relevant evidence.
01
SSRN Working Paper Dissertation Chapter 2026
Generative AI and Firm Hiring Demand: Evidence from Advanced and Developing Economies

Studies how ChatGPT's release affected firm hiring demand using global job posting data. Constructs predetermined firm-level AI exposure with a continuous difference-in-differences design. Firms primarily adjust along the composition margin, with heterogeneous dynamics across advanced and developing economies.

02
Draft Dissertation Chapter 2026
Price Discrimination and Gender Inequities in Health Insurance: Evidence from the ACA's Gender Rating Ban

Examines the impact of the ACA's gender rating ban on insurance coverage using ACS data (2010–2018). Leverages cross-state variation in pre-policy pricing practices with difference-in-differences and triple-differences designs. Results show increased coverage among women in highly exposed states, providing evidence on how anti-discrimination policy affects access to insurance.

03
Work in Progress Dissertation Chapter
Geographic Mobility and Smoking Behavior: Evidence from the PSID

Uses PSID panel data to examine how geographic mobility affects smoking through changes in local environments. Leveraging within-individual variation, fixed-effects models reveal strong evidence of exposure effects, especially during adolescence.

04
Work in Progress
Reevaluating Medicaid Expansion Using Callaway–Sant'Anna Difference-in-Differences

Revisits the effects of Medicaid expansion using modern staggered-adoption DiD estimators with CPS data.
